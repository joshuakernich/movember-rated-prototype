CONVERSATIONS

Helping you talk to the men in your life.

Who are Movember?

Movember are the leading charity changing the face of men’s health. We know what works for men – and what doesn’t.

Movember looks at mental health through a male lens, focusing on prevention, early intervention and health promotion.

We’re working towards a world where men take action to be mentally well, and are supported by those around them.

Why Conversations?

Many people don’t know what to say or worry they could make the situation worse by approaching the person. However, reaching out to someone you’re worried about is often the catalyst for that person starting on the road.

The guide explains how having a conversation can help people feel less alone and more supported in getting help for anxiety and depression, and what to do if your attempt to have the conversation is met with a bad reaction.

  • When Things Get Serious

  • WATCH

    Watch out for signs that the men around you are struggling. This includes their:

    • Losing interest in things they care about.
    • Tiredness or lethargy.
    • Taking sick days or otherwise being absent without explanation.
    • Seeming irritable or quick to take offence.
    • Any unusual changes in their behaviour of feelings.
    • Hearing them describe themselves as a burden.

    It's also wise to watch out for circumstances in a man's life that may be particularly challenging. This includes:

    • Academic pressure
    • Financial problems
    • Relationship breakdown
    • Loss of a loved one

    While many of these signs may indicate men are struggling and need support. They may also allude to more serious mental health concerns. If you are seriously concerned that someone may be a threat to themselves or others, seek additional help.

  • ASK

    Asking the right questions at the right time is vital to help a man open up.

    Consider Appropriateness

    It's important to find the right time and place to have sensitive conversations. Consider finding a somewhere private, calm, and sensitive to the needs of the individual man. Dont get hung up on everything being perfect. Consider sitting side-by-side, being delicate, and courageous.

    Use Open-ended Conversations

    Open-ended questions are a great way to stimulate meaningful conversation. This means asking paying close attention, being specific, and inquiring from a place of genuine care.

    Model Honest Conversations

    Demonstrating your own personal ability to be honest, open, and vulnerable is a great way to help a man open up. Risking exposure of your own struggles helps destigmatize the issues and makes strides toward developing a close relationship.

  • LISTEN

    Support honesty

    Good listenting starts with providing a sounding board that can be trusted. This means avoiding assumptions, avoiding judgement, and avoiding the temptation to provide answers.

    Identify clarifying questions

    Express genuine interest in understanding the man and the conversation.

    Questions like:

    • What do you mean by...
    • Are you saying...
    • Do you feel...
    • Do you mean...

  • ENCOURAGE ACTION

    Encourage Physical Well-being

    Our well-being can be drastically effected by how well we take care of our bodies. Encouraging or helping a man to take better care of himself can be a great step toward improved well-bring. You might encourage:

    • Going for a walk
    • Exercise
    • Improved sleep
    • Eating healthy

    Encourage Help-seeking

    We should not expect ourselves to be the only source of support for a friend. It is important to encourage seeking help through a wider network that may include:

    • Parents
    • School counsellers
    • Psycologists

  • CHECK-IN

    Planning a Follow-Up

    Ongoing conversation is vital in ensuring the enduring value of our connection. Once we have asked, listened, and encouraged action, always arrange to check-in with a man at a future time.

    Developing Trust

    Building a relationship and nurturing trust can be a slow process. It's important to be honest, consistent, and always maintain confidentiality.
